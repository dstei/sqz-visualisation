Upper left: Electric field in phase space, axes are real and imaginary part of E(t), where E(t) is the complex field amplitude over time.
Lower left: Re E(t) = electric field strength over time.
Upper right: Phase space rotating with the frequency of the electric field, axes are quadratures of angles 0 and π/2 (= X1 and X2).
Lower left: Electric field strength over quadrature angle.

Dark Red: (Random) complex amplitude.
Green: Electric field strength = real part of complex amplitude.
Blue: Detected quadratures = projection of complex amplitude on comoving coordinate axes.
Dashed black: Uncertainty around classical field.

Classical field sets uncertainties to zero. Coherent state sets uncertainties to 0.25. Thermal state sets uncertainties to 0.5. Quadrature-squeezed states set squeezing angle to phase and affect uncertainties. Vacuum state sets uncertainties to 0.25 and amplitude to zero. Vacuum-squeezed state affects uncertainties and sets amplitude to zero. Note that all other parameters stay unchanged. Particularly the amplitude might have to be reset to a non-zero value to properly see effects.

For code to create the aninmations, see github.com/dstei/sqz-visualisation.
